#### Cosmic Princess Kaguya! (超かぐや姫！), the 2026 Netflix original from Studio Colorido and Studio Chromato directed by Shingo Yamashita, takes the classic Japanese folktale The Tale of the Bamboo Cutter and sends it soaring into a bright, energetic future. Clocking in at roughly 142–143 minutes, this musical anime mixes virtual-reality streaming battles, dazzling idol performances, a warm friendship laced with gentle yuri undertones, and touching themes of fleeting connections and self-discovery.

![CPK](https://cdn.imgchest.com/files/8fcea13576bd.jpg)


The story centers on Iroha Sakayori, a diligent high-schooler who balances classes with part-time jobs, until her steady routine is disrupted by Kaguya, a luminous and wildly free-spirited girl from the Moon who quite literally crashes into her life. The pair dives headfirst into the glowing virtual metaverse called Tsukuyomi, where they compete as streamers and performers while confronting the painful fact that Kaguya’s stay on Earth has an expiration date. Anna Nagase gives Iroha a grounded, relatable voice that contrasts perfectly with Yuuko Natsuyoshi’s buoyant, chaotic take on Kaguya, creating a chemistry many compare to the emotional depth of Your Name or the dynamic spark in Lycoris Recoil.

The animation is breathtaking from start to finish. Dance sequences flow with effortless grace, action scenes burst with kinetic energy, and the concert moments are pure spectacle. Tsukuyomi itself feels alive, blending elegant Japanese traditional elements with sleek futuristic neon in a way that feels both nostalgic and forward-looking.

![CPK](https://cdn.imgchest.com/files/e12468c44bcb.jpg)

The soundtrack is equally captivating. Original songs and remixes pull inspiration from Vocaloid trailblazers like ryo (supercell), 40mP, and HoneyWorks, resulting in tracks that are instantly catchy yet deeply emotional. They weave seamlessly into the story’s performances, capturing the rush of creating and sharing art in the digital age. For many, the music alone is reason enough to hit replay.

At its heart, the film thrives on Iroha and Kaguya’s relationship. Their opposing personalities—Iroha’s quiet sense of duty against Kaguya’s unstoppable moon-born enthusiasm—generate humor, growth, and genuinely moving moments. Themes of coming-of-age pressures, the challenges and joys of being a creator, the comfort of found family, and the ache of temporary bonds all resonate strongly, often leaving viewers with a smile tinged with bittersweet tears.

![CPK](https://cdn.imgchest.com/files/36d625aa279f.jpg)

What makes the experience even more special is how the entire film feels like a heartfelt love letter to Vocaloid, NicoNico-era creator culture, VTubers, streamers, and the boundless possibilities of virtual reality. It celebrates the joy of digital self-expression, online performances, avatar freedom, and building communities in virtual spaces with genuine passion and optimism. The vibrant world of Tsukuyomi, the high-energy streaming battles, the idol-like concerts, and the nostalgic-yet-fresh soundtrack all come together as an enthusiastic tribute to the people who create, perform, and connect in today’s digital landscape. It’s not just retelling an old tale—it’s elevating and honoring the spirit of modern online creativity.

![CPK](https://cdn.imgchest.com/files/e52b518de040.jpg)

The film’s ambition, however, occasionally works against it. Pacing fluctuates, with some sections feeling stretched and the finale rushing to tie everything together. The third act’s revelations involving time loops and 8000-year cycles can leave viewers puzzled about the exact timeline and resolution. While it handles its blend of folktale echoes, VR competition, musical numbers, and emotional drama with impressive style, the sheer number of ideas sometimes crowds the narrative, keeping it just shy of the flawless, enduring impact of titles like Your Name or Belle.

Even so, Cosmic Princess Kaguya! remains a joyful, inventive, and visually stunning experience. It shines brightest for anyone who enjoys musical anime, virtual worlds, idol culture, or stories about pursuing dreams in today’s connected landscape. It’s charming, full of life, and well worth the watch, even if it stops short of masterpiece territory.
